Project 2 Persuasive or Deceptive Visualization?

Yidong Shi, Haoshuo Bi, Yuntao San — yis089@ucsd.edu, hbi@ucsd.edu, joshan@ucsd.edu

Proposition: Countries with higher education levels tend to have older populations.

FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Normalize both series to 0–1 (+1.5 Earnest): 统一量纲便于直观比较两条曲线的共同趋势；代价是弱化绝对量级差异。
  • Long horizon (1970–2020) (+1.0): 拉长时间窗口揭示结构性共变，减少短期噪声对结论的干扰。
  • Single shared y-scale & unobtrusive grid (+1.0): 相同尺度与克制网格降低读图成本，把注意力集中在“同步上升”。
  • Neutral framing + key-point annotations (+0.5): 用中性标题与关键节点注释引导“长期同向”的解读，而非情绪化用词。

AGAINST the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Narrow time window: 1995–2000 (−1.0 Deceptive): 选择短期时段削弱长期相关性；若不显著披露窗口选择，具有轻度欺骗性。
  • Subset to mid–high enrollment countries (−0.5): 聚焦 58–82% 区间让散点更“无序”，弱化整体正相关印象。
  • No trendline / smoothing (−0.5): 不提供回归或平滑线，增强“看不出关系”的第一眼感受。
  • Plain, cautionary title framing (+0.5): 用克制措辞提示“在窄窗口内关系看起来较弱”，避免夸张语言。

Final Reflection

Designing for both sides showed how framing alone—normalization, time-window selection, and subsetting—can pivot a reader’s takeaway without changing the dataset. On a long horizon, standardized series co-move and make the relationship feel structural; on a narrow window with a restricted subset, the same relationship looks contingent or even absent.

We define ethical visualization as disclosing transformations that materially affect interpretation and avoiding moves a reasonable reader cannot easily detect. Acceptable persuasion clarifies an argument while surfacing caveats (e.g., long-horizon trends with annotations). Misleading choices conceal those caveats or manufacture impressions that would not survive under equally reasonable views (e.g., aggressively cherry-picked windows without disclosure). Our “against” design intentionally uses a mild form of deception (narrow window + subset) to reveal the fragility of conclusions and to motivate explicit disclosure in captions and write-ups.
